Moana of Motuni

Moana is a strong-willed, independent wayfinder. Though she has moments of self-doubt, she has great pride in who she is and where she comes from, and doesn't back away from new challenges.Moana, the strong-willed daughter of a chief of a coastal village, who is chosen by the ocean itself to reunite a mystical relic with the goddess Te Fiti.

  • HeiHei

    • He is a bumbling, accident-prone rooster who is regarded as the "village idiot" of Motunui.

    • Hei Hei is much deeper than he seems. He, maybe more than even Moana, represents the story at large. He represents the scared people that Moana will soon lead while the rock he's constantly trying to eat in the movie, is the island itself. He keeps pecking away at it, unable to stop.


  • Maui

    • He is characterised as a demigod, trickster and shapeshifter

    • He distinguishable by his bulky tattooed body, thick, flowing hair and magical fish hook that allows him to shapeshift into various creatures. Maui's tattoos recount his deeds and feature an animated miniature version of himself that pokes fun at his overinflated ego.


  • Gramma Tala

    • Moana's best friend and grandmother who shares her special connection to the ocean.

    • Moana's confidante and best friend, shares her granddaughter's special connection to the ocean. Although her son, Chief Tui, is a no-nonsense leader, Gramma Tala most definitely dances to the beat of her own drum. She feels in her soul that the ocean has big plans for Moana, but she won't reveal the legendary secrets of their wayfinding ancestors till the time is right.
